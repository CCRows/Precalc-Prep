Free Response: where 4s are made and lost.

Each FRQ = 6 points. To hit a 4 you typically need 14-16 raw FRQ points combined with 24+/40 MCQ. Each FRQ has a predictable structure — learn the 4 types, you know what is coming.

The 4 FRQ Types (always in this order)
FRQ 1 · 6 pts

Function Concepts

CALCULATOR

Read graphs/tables, compute average rate of change, identify function behavior (inc/dec, concavity).

  • Show ARC quotient: (f(b) - f(a))/(b - a)
  • Round to 3 decimal places
  • Justify behavior using table values
FRQ 2 · 6 pts

Modeling: Non-Periodic

CALCULATOR

Build a polynomial, exponential, or log model from data. Interpret in context with units.

  • Set up equations from data points
  • Use calc regression if asked
  • ALWAYS include units
FRQ 3 · 6 pts

Modeling: Periodic

NO CALC

Sinusoidal modeling. Find a, b, c, d in f(x) = a sin(b(x-c)) + d.

  • Amp = (max - min)/2
  • Midline = (max + min)/2
  • b = 2pi / period
FRQ 4 · 6 pts

Symbolic Manipulation

NO CALC

Algebra-heavy. Solve log/exp equations, rewrite trig, prove identities.

  • Use exact values (no decimals)
  • Show every step
  • Use parentheses around log args
TASK VERB DECODER
Determine / Find: Show work, give numerical answer.
Justify / Explain: Words required. State the rule + cite the data.
Describe: Use math vocabulary (increasing, concave up, etc).
Construct: Build the equation/model from scratch.
Interpret: Answer in context with units.

Exam Day Strategy

Knowing the math is half the battle. Knowing exam tactics is the other half. Read this BEFORE 5/21.

Pacing
  • MCQ Part A (no calc): 28 questions · 80 min · ~3 min each
  • MCQ Part B (calc): 12 questions · 40 min · ~3 min each
  • FRQ Part A (calc): 2 questions · 30 min · 15 min each
  • FRQ Part B (no calc): 2 questions · 30 min · 15 min each
THE 60% STRATEGY (your target for a 4)
Do not try to get every question right — that is how you panic and run out of time. Aim to NAIL ~28 MCQ confidently and grab partial credit on every FRQ part you can. Skip the hardest 2-3 MCQ if needed and come back. Never leave a blank — there is no penalty for guessing.
MCQ Strategy
  • NEVER leave a blank. No penalty for wrong answers. Even random guess = 25% chance.
  • If stuck after 30 sec, mark and move on. Come back at the end.
  • For all of the following EXCEPT questions, eliminate the 3 that DO work.
  • For function questions, plug in test values (x=0, x=1, x=-1) to eliminate options.
  • Use your calculator on Part B for graphing, regression, and equation solving — do not waste time doing algebra by hand.
FRQ Strategy
  • Show all work. Correct answer with no work often = 0 points. Work alone often = partial credit.
  • Read ALL parts before starting. Sometimes part (c) is independent of (a) — do what you can.
  • Box your final answers so the reader can find them.
  • Include UNITS on every interpretation question. Units = points.
  • If a part says justify: write a sentence. Do not just state the answer.
  • For decimals: 3 decimal places unless told otherwise.
  • For exact (no calc): use exact values like sqrt(3)/2, never decimal approximations.
Night Before
  • Do not cram new material — review your formula notes and unit circle for 30 min max.
  • Pack your bag: 2+ pencils, eraser, approved graphing calculator with FRESH BATTERIES, ID, water bottle.
  • Set 2 alarms. Eat a real breakfast (protein + carbs).
  • In bed by 10pm. Sleep beats one more hour of cramming.
Exam Morning
  • Arrive 15 min early.
  • Set calculator to RADIAN mode the moment you open it.
  • If your mind goes blank on a question, breathe, skip it, come back. Confidence comes back when you nail the next one.
  • Trust your prep. You have done the work. Now just execute.
